The Fresh Knowledge Filter

A shadow AI hiring scenario. Watch for the moments a governance framework should have caught this.

FOR TRAINING USE
The setupScene 1 / 6
A high-volume hiring season

Applications for a Senior Analyst role are way up. The company's approved AI screening tool is helping — but it's slow.

The workaroundScene 2 / 6
A faster, unapproved tool

Frustrated, someone on the HR team redacts identifying info and batch-uploads applications to a faster tool — one that was never approved.

STOP

"What's the first red flag here — before we even know the outcome?"

Quick call-out, 60 seconds. No wrong answers yet.

WHAT →Shadow AI. An unapproved tool was used outside the governance process — no one checked the tool or the prompt first.

ALSO →Data was redacted, but the prompt itself was never reviewed for risky language.

What you noticeScene 3 / 6
Almost everyone interviewed is under 35

The team pulls the full applicant pool to compare against who actually got screened out.

The patternScene 4 / 6
Qualified. Filtered out anyway.

Candidates with 15–20 years of directly relevant experience — some using the company's current tech stack for a decade — never made it past round one.

STOP

"No one asked the AI to filter by age. So where did this actually go wrong in our framework?"

Quick call-out, 60–90 seconds.

HOW →No Process Flow Diagram step existed to review prompts or audit outputs before decisions were made.

WHAT →An unapproved tool never went through vetting that might have caught this.

But waitScene 5 / 6
Nobody coded "age." The model found a proxy for it.

"Fresh knowledge" was meant to mean recent hands-on skill. The model read it as recency of education — graduation year was the closest signal it had.

STOP

"If you owned this program, what's the one framework piece you'd point to first — Who, What, or How?"

Any defensible answer works — push for one concrete fix.

WHO →HR/Legal and the Core AI Team should own review of hiring prompts, not an individual, in the moment.

WHAT →The tool should never have been usable without Shadow AI approval first.

HOW →A process flow with a bias-check / human-review gate before any candidate is filtered out.

DebriefScene 6 / 6
One missing piece, three points of failure
Who

Ownership was undefined in the moment — no one felt it was their job to flag a new tool or review the prompt.

What

Shadow AI: a well-intentioned workaround bypassed the approved toolkit entirely.

How

No process step existed to catch proxy bias before it reached real candidates.

The AI didn't need to be told to discriminate by age — it found the nearest signal to a fuzzy instruction. That's exactly what a governance framework exists to catch.
